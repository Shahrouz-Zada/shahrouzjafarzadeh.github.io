Research & Working Projects

Rolling Fractal Dimension as an Early-Warning Signal for Market Regime Shifts

Working Paper — In Progress

This paper develops the Rolling Fractal Dimension (RFD) as a real-time measure of price-path geometric complexity. It investigates whether increases in local irregularity systematically precede volatility spikes, drawdowns, and structural breaks. The framework is benchmarked against standard risk indicators including realized volatility, GARCH-type models, Hurst-based metrics, entropy measures, and change-point detection methods.

Aim: to provide a simple, interpretable early-warning framework for detecting market regime shifts.

A Quantitative Antifragility Index for Financial Markets

Research Project — Early Stage

This ongoing project aims to construct a unified, data-driven Antifragility Index for financial markets. The proposed index combines complexity-based features such as fractal and multifractal measures, long-memory statistics, and recurrence-based diagnostics. Its empirical value is evaluated through predictive frameworks including local projections and logistic hazard models across multiple asset classes, including equities, foreign exchange, and crypto-assets.

Aim: to build a measurable and interpretable antifragility metric that improves risk forecasting and regime-shift diagnostics.

Towards Antifragility in Financial Markets: A Fractal Geometry and Machine Learning Framework

Doctoral Dissertation

This dissertation examined how antifragile behaviour and regime instability can be detected and modelled using data-driven, complexity-based methods. The work integrated fractal and multifractal analysis, recurrence quantification, Hurst-based measures, feature engineering, and machine learning models including LSTM, Random Forest, and SVM.

Key findings include strong fractal and multifractal behaviour during crisis periods, evidence that rolling fractal measures rise ahead of volatility transitions, and improved predictive performance from fractal-enhanced machine learning models in turbulent regimes. This dissertation provides the theoretical and empirical foundation for the ongoing projects above.
